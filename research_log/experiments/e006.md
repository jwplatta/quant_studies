## Change Tested

Tested experiment 6 from the repair matrix: when a live 7DTE SPXW iron condor
reaches short-leg delta `>= 0.25` or breaches a short strike while in the `1-2`
DTE bucket before `12:00` ET, the strategy closes the position and reopens a
fresh condor on the next valid expiry.

## Method

Ran the standalone project `qc/spxw_7dte_exp06_roll_late_am` in QuantConnect
cloud and parsed the saved results payload from
`research_log/results/spxw_7dte_exp06_roll_late_am.json`. The project used
minute SPX and SPXW data, baseline entry constants, and the same
`IronCondorFinder` as the no-constraints baseline.

## Interpretation

This is the strongest repair result in the sequence so far on headline PnL, but
it still does not clear the bar. The rule materially reduced the net loss versus
the earlier mid-DTE variants, yet it did so with very high turnover and still
left the portfolio with negative risk-adjusted performance and a substantial
drawdown. On these results, late-cycle roll-forward looks directionally better
than the prior fixes but not robust enough to accept as a production repair.
