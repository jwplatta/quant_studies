## Change Tested

This run tests the simplest repair response: when the common stress trigger fires
on a live 7DTE SPXW iron condor, the strategy closes the entire condor and does
not reopen.

## Interpretation

The hard-exit rule did exactly what the hypothesis expected on tail control. It
did not create positive expectancy, but it transformed the risk profile from an
almost total-capital-loss baseline into a materially more survivable process.
The remaining question is whether more adaptive repairs can preserve some of this
drawdown improvement while retaining more upside than an immediate full exit.
