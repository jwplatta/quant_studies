## Change Tested

Tested experiment 5 from the repair matrix: when a live 7DTE SPXW iron condor
reaches short-leg delta `>= 0.20` while in the `3-5` DTE bucket, the strategy
closes the position and reopens a fresh condor on the next valid expiry.

## Method

Ran the standalone project `qc/spxw_7dte_exp05_roll_mid_dte` in QuantConnect
cloud and parsed the saved results payload from
`research_log/results/spxw_7dte_exp05_roll_mid_dte.json`. The project used
minute SPX and SPXW data, baseline entry constants, and the same
`IronCondorFinder` as the no-constraints baseline.

## Interpretation

This was the best of the three mid-DTE variants tested so far, but that only
means it lost less badly. Relative to `exp03`, it avoided the catastrophic
blow-up, and relative to `exp04`, it reduced both net loss and drawdown.
That still leaves a clearly negative result with weak path quality, so the
mid-DTE roll-forward rule does not meet the bar for a consistent repair.
