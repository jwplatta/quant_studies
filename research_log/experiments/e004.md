## Change Tested

Tested experiment 4 from the repair matrix: when a live 7DTE SPXW iron condor
reaches short-leg delta `>= 0.20` while in the `3-5` DTE bucket, the strategy
closes the condor outright.

## Method

Ran the standalone project `qc/spxw_7dte_exp04_close_mid_dte` in QuantConnect
cloud and parsed the saved results payload from
`research_log/results/spxw_7dte_exp04.json`. The project used minute SPX and
SPXW data, baseline entry constants, and the same `IronCondorFinder` as the
no-constraints baseline.

## Interpretation

This variant was materially worse than the high-DTE close test and still did
not show evidence of tail improvement at the portfolio level. Closing in the
middle bucket appears to lock in too much damage while still leaving the
strategy with a poor return path and large drawdown. On these results, the
mid-DTE hard-close idea should be treated as rejected.
