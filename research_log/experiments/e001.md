## Change Tested

Tested experiment 1 from the repair matrix: when a live 7DTE SPXW iron condor
reaches short-leg delta `>= 0.20` while it is still in the `6-7` DTE bucket,
the strategy closes the position and re-enters a fresh iron condor on the same
expiry using the baseline `IronCondorFinder`.

## Method

Ran the standalone project `qc/spxw_7dte_exp01_recenter_high_dte` in
QuantConnect cloud and parsed the saved results payload from
`research_log/results/spxw_7dte_exp01_recenter_high_dte_20260330_retry1.json`.
The project used minute SPX and SPXW data, baseline entry constants, and the
same `IronCondorFinder` as the no-constraints baseline.

## Interpretation

This variant avoided an outright blow-up and produced a modest positive net
return, but the negative Sharpe and Sortino ratios suggest the path quality was
poor relative to the volatility taken. On a first pass, this looks more like a
rule that preserved some expectancy than one that clearly improved the left
tail. The real question is whether its worst losses, drawdown path, and loss
concentration improved versus experiment 0.
