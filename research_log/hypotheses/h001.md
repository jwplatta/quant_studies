## Raw Question

Which isolated repair policy, if any, reduces large-loss behavior relative to
`qc/spxw_7dte_no_constraints` when the baseline 7DTE SPXW iron condor is traded
from January 1, 2022 through December 31, 2025 on minute data?

## Notes

- Actor: A single-position 7DTE SPXW iron condor strategy using the baseline `IronCondorFinder`.
- Constraint: Hold the baseline entry cash, DTE, pricing, and order-refresh constants fixed.
- Mechanism: Test only one repair trigger and one repair action per project folder.
- Persistence: The rule should work across multiple market regimes, not just isolated crash windows.
- Kill condition: Repairs reduce average PnL or trade frequency but do not improve drawdown or left-tail concentration.

## Primary Prediction

Rules that add time or recenter earlier in the lifecycle will have a better
chance of reducing extreme losses than late close-only rules.

## Secondary Predictions

- High-DTE close-only logic will reduce some tail exposure but likely give up too much expectancy.
- Mid-DTE roll-forward logic should outperform mid-DTE recenter logic if time extension matters more than spot re-centering.
- Late-cycle close rules may help headline drawdown but can arrive too late to materially improve the worst losses.
