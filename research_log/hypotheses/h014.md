## Raw Question

When a 7DTE condor is under pressure, does resetting the strike location on the same
expiry improve outcomes, or does it just realize the first loss and reload exposure?

## Notes

- Actor: The shared 7DTE SPXW iron condor engine with a same-expiry recenter repair.
- Constraint: No DTE bucket logic, time-of-day filtering, or strategy-specific entry tuning is allowed.
- Mechanism: On trigger, close the current condor and re-enter a fresh symmetric condor centered around current price on the same expiry.
- Persistence: Any benefit should appear in repeated adverse paths where the original strikes become too directional.
- Kill condition: Recenter behaves like a loss reset that increases turnover without reducing worst-case post-trigger outcomes relative to baseline or hard exit.

## Primary Prediction

Recenter should improve some medium-stress outcomes relative to baseline by removing
the original tested strikes, but it may still leave meaningful tail exposure because
expiry does not move outward.

## Secondary Predictions

- Recenter may outperform hard exit on average trigger-to-final PnL if enough repaired trades stabilize after re-entry.
- The strategy will likely show more trade churn and more logged trigger/re-entry sequences than the other variants.

Run `qc/spxw_7dte_recenter` and compare it against baseline and hard exit with
